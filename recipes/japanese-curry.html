Japanese Curry

Description

Japanese curry is gaining popularity around the globe as a top-notch soul food. It's almost like a hybrid of a traditional beef stew and Indian curry, making use of several different spices for maximum flavor and served on a bed of rice.

Most homes throughout the nation have their own special way of making it that takes a standard supermarket roux and elevates it with secret ingredients. Our recipe is no different, making use of instant coffee, apples, and more. Read on for tips on how to make the ultimate Japanese curry.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp frying oil (vegetable, olive, etc.)
  • 400g of cubed beef shoulder
  • 1 medium sized onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, chopped to 1/4 inch thickness
  • 2 russet potatoes, cubed
  • 800 ml water
  • 1 box of Golden Curry roux
  • 1 tsp instant coffee
  • 1/2 of a gala apple, diced to 1/4 inch cubes
  1. Add oil to a hot pot over medium heat and fry onions until translucent, 4-5 minutes.
  2. Add the cubed beef shoulder and brown the meat.
  3. Add the water, carrots, potatoes, and any other ingredients you desire. Let the water come to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Break up the roux and add it, along with the remaining ingredients, to the pot. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes, or until the desired consistency is achieved.
  5. Serve over a bed of your favorite rice.
