Okayama Scramble

Home

Description

The fabled Okayama scramble, invented in the prefecture neighboring Hiroshima from which it got its name, took years to perfect. First there were bell peppers, then piman. Then there was shreaded mozza and cheddar before I landed on parmesan. Occasionally I'll add shiitake mushrooms or eringi.

You can mix and match whatever ingredients you want to, but these are the ones that I recommend for a guaranteed hit that'll start any weekend off right. It combines smoke, spice, and umami for a breakfast made in heaven.

Ingredients

  • 1 russet potato, cut into 3/4 inch cubes
  • 1/4 of a whole white onion, sliced into thin lengths
  • 1 Japanese piman, diced
  • 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 1 cup chorizo sausage, diced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp mixed italian
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup shaved parmesan
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in pan and fry potatoes until golden brown.
  2. Add onion and piman and fry for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the seasoning and mix well.
  4. Add the sausages to the main frypan. In a separate pan, cook the scrambled eggs. Add to the main frypan when ready.
  5. Once the sausages are cooked, sprinkle shaved parmesan on top and serve once melted.
